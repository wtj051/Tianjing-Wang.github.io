Teaching

Teaching energy systems through physics, data, and responsible decision-making

My teaching connects first-principles reasoning, computational modeling, machine learning, optimization, and responsible engineering practice for power grids, batteries, electric mobility, and data-center energy infrastructure.

Teaching philosophy

From equations to decisions

I aim to help students move from understanding physical laws to building computational tools that make safe and accountable decisions. In energy courses, students should learn not only how to optimize a system, but also how to identify when a model is unsafe, non-reproducible, or inconsistent with physical constraints.

Teaching principles

  • Start with physical intuition and system constraints.
  • Translate theory into simulation and reproducible code.
  • Evaluate models by failure modes, not only accuracy.
  • Connect assignments to real energy-system decisions.
