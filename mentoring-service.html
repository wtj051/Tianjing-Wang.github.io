Mentoring & Service

Mentoring

I have supervised or co-supervised Ph.D. students on battery degradation prediction under stochastic discharge conditions and virtual power plant scheduling under extreme weather. My mentoring philosophy emphasizes intellectual ownership, structured feedback, reproducible research practice, and career development.

Mentoring practices

  • Project charters defining scientific questions, datasets, metrics, risks, and publication plans.
  • Weekly technical meetings and regular individual development check-ins.
  • Shared templates for literature reviews, experiment logs, code review, and reproducibility checklists.
  • Conference preparation, paper-writing support, and active sponsorship for awards, talks, and collaborations.

Academic service

  • Guest Editor, IEEE Transactions on Consumer Electronics.
  • Topical Advisory Panel Member and Guest Editor, Energies.
  • Reviewer for IEEE Transactions on Industrial Informatics, IEEE Transactions on Smart Grid, Applied Energy, and related energy-AI venues.
  • IEEE Power & Energy Society General Meeting participation and student poster review.
