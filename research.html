Research

My research develops trustworthy, physics-guided AI for power systems, battery intelligence, energy storage, and grid-battery-mobility coordination. The central question is:

How can AI make fast decisions for clean-energy infrastructure while respecting physical feasibility, device health, privacy, uncertainty, and operational accountability?

Theme 1: Physics-trustworthy AI for safe grid operation

I develop AI methods that embed power-system constraints into learning and control models, including power balance, power-flow solvability, voltage/security limits, transient stability, dispatch rules, and expert knowledge.

Representative directions:

  • Graph/time-series reinforcement learning for power-flow solvability restoration.
  • Transfer reinforcement learning for security-constrained redispatch across heterogeneous grids.
  • Topology-aware and knowledge-informed emergency-control models.
  • Physics-based safety metrics for AI agents and energy foundation models.

Theme 2: Life-aware battery intelligence

Battery health estimates become operationally useful when they are translated into safe capacity, power boundaries, efficiency, degradation cost, and dispatch constraints. My work connects SOH/SOC/RUL/future-capacity estimation with energy-storage operation and grid-edge decision support.

Representative directions:

  • Physics-guided deep learning for future capacity and remaining useful life prediction.
  • Continuous-time degradation modeling under irregular sampling and missing data.
  • Weakly supervised SOH estimation under imprecise intervals.
  • Health-to-dispatch battery toolboxes for lifecycle-aware storage flexibility.

Theme 3: Privacy-preserving and collaborative energy intelligence

Energy data are distributed across utilities, charging operators, vehicles, storage owners, and users. My work develops learning and decision systems that coordinate system-level intelligence with local constraints while reducing the need to centralize raw data.

Representative directions:

  • Federated and privacy-preserving learning for smart grids and battery health estimation.
  • Big-small model coordination for privacy-constrained multi-agent energy systems.
  • Digital-twin validation under extreme weather, missing data, renewable forecast errors, EV charging peaks, and device degradation.

Future lab vision: Trustworthy Energy Intelligence Lab

My future lab will develop physics-guided AI, energy foundation models, and privacy-preserving decision systems that enable power grids, batteries, electric vehicles, and distributed storage to operate safely, sustainably, and collaboratively under uncertainty.
