Benchmark

TrustEnergyBench

A public benchmark for evaluating trustworthy AI agents and physics-guided foundation-model components in critical energy systems.

GitHub repository Benchmark tracks Release roadmap
Benchmark thesis. Energy AI should be evaluated not only by prediction accuracy, but also by physical feasibility, constraint violations, uncertainty stress tests, battery lifetime impact, grid-service value, and reproducibility.

Why this benchmark?

Making failure modes measurable

Purely data-driven energy-AI models can violate power-flow equations, voltage/security limits, SOC/SOH boundaries, degradation-risk budgets, and grid-service constraints under distribution shift. At the same time, AI-driven data centers introduce fast-growing flexible demand that should be modeled jointly with storage, cooling, renewable supply, and grid constraints.

Inputs

  • AI workload and computing-demand scenarios
  • Electricity price and renewable uncertainty
  • On-site or grid-edge battery constraints
  • Cooling/thermal flexibility and service-quality limits
  • Grid-stress or interconnection signals

Outputs

  • Workload-shifting or demand-flexibility decisions
  • Battery charge/discharge schedules
  • Peak-shaving or grid-support responses
  • Risk scores under uncertainty

Metrics

  • Total energy and demand cost
  • Peak-load and grid-stress reduction
  • Renewable utilization and carbon-aware operation
  • Service-quality violation rate
  • Battery degradation and lifetime impact
